Urban Interactive Maps

Click here to see my final project from Geography 575: Interactive Mapping and GeoVisualization. It is a hypothetical newspaper discussing the new and current rapid transit in the Twin Cities, with respect to demographics. I worked with two other undergrads in the class using Leaflet.js to create a comprehensive tool for exploration, complete with a geosearch, dozens of choropleth layers and toggle buttons for current and proposed rapid transit lines. Note: There have been a few reported errors while viewing in chrome.

Click here to see a map I made with MapboxGL.js. It uses proportional symbols to show murders on the south side of Chicago from 2008 to 2014. I recreated a map I had already made in Leaflet.js to teach myself MapboxGL.js as part of an independant study.
